PROJECT 5 • R PROGRAMMING • HOUSING ANALYTICS

UK Housing Costs vs Income — Hypothesis Testing & Regression

This project explores the relationship between income and housing cost using R. It begins with data loading, cleaning, exploration, summary analysis, and visualisation, before progressing into statistical analysis including correlation and regression modelling. The final stage focuses on interpreting results and applying insights to real-world housing affordability decisions.

R ggplot2 dplyr readr Housing Data EDA Regression

📌 Project Overview

This project examines housing affordability by analysing housing cost, income, employment status, property type, and region within a UK housing dataset. The work completed so far focuses on data loading, data quality checks, cleaning, descriptive analysis, grouping, and visualisation. The findings provide an exploratory foundation for the final stage, which will involve statistical testing and regression.

What the Project Covers

Data exploration, missing value checks, duplicate handling, cleaning of text fields, grouping by categories, and visualisation of patterns in housing cost and income.

Dataset Used

A UK housing income dataset containing region, employment status, property type, education, marital status, age, income, housing cost, savings, and record date.

Project Goal

To understand how income relates to housing cost, identify patterns across categories, and prepare the dataset for advanced statistical testing and modelling.

6️⃣ Final Insights & Data-Driven Recommendations

Key Insights
  • Income has a statistically significant positive relationship with housing cost.
  • The Correlation (r = 0.661) confirms a strong linear association between the two variables.
  • The regression model explains approximately 43.7% of housing cost variation (R² = 0.437).
  • Housing cost distribution is right-skewed, indicating the presence of high-cost outliers.
  • Regional differences exist but are relatively small compared to income influence.
  • Property type and employment status contribute additional variation beyond income.
Data-Driven Recommendations
  • Incorporate additional predictors (region, property type, savings) to improve model accuracy.
  • Use multiple regression to better capture real-world housing affordability factors.
  • Monitor and handle outliers carefully to avoid distortion in model performance.
  • Combine statistical findings with domain knowledge when making housing-related decisions.
  • Use insights to inform affordability analysis, policy discussions, and financial planning.
