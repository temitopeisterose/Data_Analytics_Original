📊 Social Media Engagement Analytics Dashboard

This project analyses social media engagement across multiple platforms using Tableau. The dashboard explores likes, shares, comments, total engagement, engagement rate, content performance, campaign performance, audience age groups, and geographic trends. The project also demonstrates data cleaning, grouping, calculated fields, dashboard design, and interactive storytelling.

Insights and Recommendations

The dashboard provides a complete view of social media engagement performance after the dataset was cleaned, grouped and transformed in Tableau. These findings are based on the KPI cards, platform comparison, campaign analysis, content type visuals, engagement trend, geographic map, heatmap, scatter plot and final dashboard filters.

Key Insights

📌 1. Strong total engagement performance

The dashboard reports a total engagement score of 27,455,226. This shows that the cleaned dataset contains a high level of audience interaction and is suitable for campaign, content and platform-level decision-making.

📌 2. Likes are the largest engagement contributor

Total likes reached 22,477,013, making likes the dominant engagement measure in the dashboard. This suggests that quick user reactions were more frequent than deeper actions such as shares or comments.

📌 3. Share activity shows strong distribution potential

Total shares reached 2,936,225. This indicates that many posts were not only liked but also shared, helping the content reach wider audiences beyond the original viewers.

📌 4. Average engagement rate became more reliable after cleaning

The average engagement rate is 17.39 after invalid values were cleaned. Removing values below 0 and above 100 helped prevent unrealistic records from distorting the KPI and visual analysis.

📌 5. Platform performance is not equal

The Likes by Platform chart shows clear differences between platforms. Some platforms generated stronger likes and engagement than others, showing that platform choice has a major impact on social media results.

📌 6. Campaign type affects engagement

The Engagement by Campaign chart and Campaign Bubble Chart show that campaign categories performed differently. Community-focused and product-related campaigns appear more effective than weaker campaign categories.

📌 7. Content format matters

The Content Type Share and Engagement Treemap visuals show that different content formats contribute differently to engagement. This means content strategy should not treat all formats as equal.

📌 8. Engagement changes over time

The Engagement Trend line chart shows that engagement fluctuates across time. This highlights the importance of monitoring campaign periods, posting frequency and timing.

📌 9. Followers do not always guarantee stronger engagement

The Followers vs Engagement scatter plot shows that a larger follower base does not always translate into higher engagement. Content quality, audience relevance and campaign message also matter.

📌 10. Geography influences campaign performance

The country map shows differences in engagement across locations. This supports the need for country-level targeting rather than using one generic strategy for every region.

📌 11. Audience age groups respond differently

The Age Group Engagement highlight table shows that engagement varies by age group and platform. This means future campaigns should be tailored to audience demographics.

📌 12. Data cleaning improved the final dashboard

Grouping inconsistent platform, content type, campaign and country values made the dashboard much cleaner. Filtering blank values also prevented misleading null categories from appearing in the visuals.

Recommendations

🚀 1. Prioritise high-performing platforms

Future campaigns should focus more resources on platforms that generated stronger likes and engagement. These platforms should receive more frequent posts, better campaign assets and closer monitoring.

🚀 2. Expand successful campaign types

Campaign types that performed strongly should be repeated and improved. Community engagement and product-related campaigns can be used as templates for future planning.

🚀 3. Invest in stronger content formats

The organisation should increase the use of high-performing content formats such as reels, videos, carousels and short-form posts where they show stronger engagement.

🚀 4. Improve weaker content types

Lower-performing content formats should be reviewed rather than removed immediately. They can be tested with better captions, stronger visuals, clearer calls to action and improved posting times.

🚀 5. Use age-group targeting

Age-group insights should guide campaign messaging. Younger audiences may respond better to fast visual content, while older groups may prefer informative or trust-building campaign messages.

🚀 6. Apply country-level campaign planning

Countries with stronger engagement should be prioritised for campaign expansion. Countries with weaker engagement should be reviewed for localisation, language, timing or platform issues.

🚀 7. Monitor engagement trends monthly

The engagement trend should be reviewed regularly to identify peaks, drops and seasonal patterns. This will help teams plan campaign launches during stronger engagement periods.

🚀 8. Do not rely only on follower count

The scatter plot shows that followers alone are not enough to measure success. Engagement rate, total engagement, campaign type and content format should be analysed together.

🚀 9. Improve data collection standards

The cleaning stage showed the importance of consistent naming. Future datasets should use standard labels for platform, campaign, content type and country fields before analysis begins.

🚀 10. Use the dashboard for stakeholder reporting

The final Tableau dashboard should be used as a reporting tool for marketing teams and managers because it brings together KPIs, charts, maps, filters and business insights in one place.
